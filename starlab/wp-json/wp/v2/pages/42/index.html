Example Scenario: SoS for Mass Casaulty Incident (MCI)<\/strong><\/span><\/p>\n

MCI<\/strong> is an\u00a0incident in which medical service resources are overwhelmed by lots of casualties (e.g.: Tianjin explosions, SARS, Daegu subway fire, etc.)<\/p>\n

Key entities are,<\/p>\n

    \n
  • Goal of SoS: save patients as many as possible<\/li>\n
  • Constituent system (CS): PTS (Patients Transferring System), hospital, etc.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    <\/p>\n

     <\/p>\n

    So, our simplified MCI scenario is like this.<\/p>\n

      \n
    • Goal of CS: PTS is to save more patients in the non-MCI area.<\/li>\n
    • Goal of SoS: SoS is to save more patients in the whole area.<\/li>\n
    • Suddenly, 200 patients are randomly occurred in the MCI area, and 50 patients are randomly occurred in the non-MCI area.<\/li>\n
    • Each patient will be either cared by the PTS or dead with a certain probability.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

      Our modeling subjects are divided into MCI and non-MCI, single CS (PTS) and each patient.<\/p>\n

      Patents have three states, occurred, cared, or dead.<\/p>\n
